# Requirements Document

## Introduction

This spec delivers the `BreakoutScene` — the third and final game mode in the paddle arcade. A single player controls a bottom paddle to bounce a ball into a grid of bricks. Bricks are destroyed on contact, scoring points. The player wins by clearing all bricks and loses when all three lives are gone. The scene uses Phaser Arcade Physics, delegates brick-grid generation and life/win/loss logic to existing pure rule modules, emits typed events to the React shell via EventBridge, and triggers audio cues for all gameplay events. Ball speed increase on paddle hit is configurable (enabled by default).

## Glossary

- **BreakoutScene**: The Phaser 3 scene class (`src/game/scenes/BreakoutScene.ts`) that runs a Breakout match using Arcade Physics.
- **Ball**: A circular Arcade Physics body that moves continuously, bouncing off the Paddle, Walls, and Bricks.
- **Paddle**: A rectangular Arcade Physics body at the bottom of the play area, controlled by keyboard input, constrained to horizontal movement.
- **Wall**: Immovable Arcade Physics bodies at the top, left, and right edges of the play area that the Ball bounces off.
- **Brick**: A rectangular Arcade Physics body in the brick grid that is destroyed on Ball collision.
- **Brick_Grid**: The collection of Brick bodies generated by the `generateBrickGrid` pure rule.
- **EventBridge**: The typed singleton event bus (`src/game/systems/EventBridge.ts`) for bidirectional Phaser ↔ React communication.
- **SceneLaunchPayload**: The typed object (`src/game/types/payload.ts`) passed from React to Phaser when launching a scene, containing match settings.
- **Life_Rules**: The existing pure functions in `src/game/rules/life-rules.ts` that manage lives, score, and match status.
- **BreakoutState**: The pure state object tracking lives, bricksRemaining, and score — managed by Life_Rules functions.
- **MatchStatus**: The result of `getMatchStatus()` — one of `'in-progress'`, `'win'`, or `'loss'`.
- **Base_Speed**: The initial Ball velocity magnitude when served.
- **Speed_Increment**: The amount Ball speed increases on each Paddle hit (when speed increase is enabled).
- **Max_Speed**: The upper bound on Ball speed to prevent tunneling.
- **Serve_Delay**: The pause (500ms–1000ms) between a life loss and the next serve, allowing the player to prepare.
- **Points_Per_Brick**: The score value awarded for each Brick destroyed (10 points).

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Scene Initialization

**User Story:** As a gameplay implementer, I want BreakoutScene to initialize from a SceneLaunchPayload via the SceneLauncher pattern, so that the scene receives validated settings from the React shell.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN BreakoutScene starts, THE BreakoutScene SHALL read the SceneLaunchPayload from the SceneLauncher module in its `init()` method as the primary data source.
2. WHEN BreakoutScene starts, THE BreakoutScene SHALL create a Paddle, a Ball, left/right/top Walls, and a Brick_Grid using Phaser Arcade Physics.
3. THE BreakoutScene SHALL position the Paddle centered horizontally near the bottom of the play area.
4. THE BreakoutScene SHALL position the Ball on top of the Paddle before the first serve.
5. IF the SceneLaunchPayload is missing or malformed, THEN THE BreakoutScene SHALL use default settings (powerupsEnabled false, speed increase enabled).
6. WHEN BreakoutScene starts, THE BreakoutScene SHALL initialize BreakoutState using `createInitialState` with the total brick count from the generated grid.

### Requirement 2: Brick Grid Creation

**User Story:** As a player, I want a grid of bricks displayed at the top of the play area, so that I have targets to clear.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN BreakoutScene creates the play area, THE BreakoutScene SHALL call `generateBrickGrid` with a configuration that fits within the play area bounds.
2. THE BreakoutScene SHALL create one Arcade Physics static body for each BrickDescriptor returned by `generateBrickGrid`.
3. FOR ALL generated Brick bodies, THE BreakoutScene SHALL ensure no Brick overlaps another Brick or extends outside the play area (grid generation invariant).
4. THE BreakoutScene SHALL render each Brick as a colored rectangle using programmatic Phaser graphics.

### Requirement 3: Paddle Movement

**User Story:** As a player, I want to control the paddle with ArrowLeft/ArrowRight and A/D keys, so that I can intercept the ball.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE the match is active, THE BreakoutScene SHALL move the Paddle left when ArrowLeft or A is held.
2. WHILE the match is active, THE BreakoutScene SHALL move the Paddle right when ArrowRight or D is held.
3. THE BreakoutScene SHALL constrain the Paddle to remain fully within the horizontal play area bounds (between left Wall and right Wall).
4. WHEN no movement key is held, THE BreakoutScene SHALL stop the Paddle's horizontal movement.
5. THE BreakoutScene SHALL use `window.addEventListener` for keyboard input to ensure reliable input in the React-embedded Phaser game.
6. WHEN BreakoutScene shuts down, THE BreakoutScene SHALL remove all window keyboard event listeners.

### Requirement 4: Ball Physics

**User Story:** As a player, I want the ball to bounce off the paddle, walls, and bricks realistically, so that gameplay feels responsive.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Ball collides with the Paddle, THE BreakoutScene SHALL reflect the Ball's velocity (bounce).
2. WHEN the Ball collides with a Wall, THE BreakoutScene SHALL reflect the Ball's velocity (bounce).
3. WHEN the Ball collides with a Brick, THE BreakoutScene SHALL reflect the Ball's velocity (bounce).
4. THE Ball SHALL maintain constant speed magnitude between collisions (no deceleration from physics drag).
5. THE BreakoutScene SHALL set `ball.body.setMaxSpeed(MAX_SPEED)` to prevent tunneling at high velocities.
6. THE BreakoutScene SHALL disable world bounds collision on the Ball and detect bottom-edge exit manually.

### Requirement 5: Ball Speed Increase (Configurable)

**User Story:** As a player, I want the ball to optionally speed up on paddle hits, so that gameplay becomes progressively more challenging.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN ball speed increase is enabled AND the Ball collides with the Paddle, THE BreakoutScene SHALL increase the Ball's speed by Speed_Increment (capped at Max_Speed).
2. WHEN ball speed increase is disabled AND the Ball collides with the Paddle, THE BreakoutScene SHALL maintain the Ball's current speed without increase.
3. WHEN a life is lost, THE BreakoutScene SHALL reset the Ball's speed to Base_Speed for the next serve.
4. FOR ALL paddle hit sequences with speed increase enabled, THE Ball's speed SHALL remain in the range [Base_Speed, Max_Speed] inclusive (speed bounds invariant).
5. THE BreakoutScene SHALL default ball speed increase to enabled.

### Requirement 6: Brick Destruction

**User Story:** As a player, I want bricks to be destroyed when the ball hits them, so that I can progress toward clearing the grid.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Ball collides with a Brick, THE BreakoutScene SHALL destroy that Brick (remove from physics world and display).
2. WHEN a Brick is destroyed, THE BreakoutScene SHALL call `breakBrick` on the current BreakoutState with Points_Per_Brick to update score and bricksRemaining.
3. WHEN a Brick is destroyed, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit a `score:update` event via EventBridge with the updated score (using `{ left: score, right: 0 }` format for compatibility).
4. WHEN a Brick is destroyed, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:brick-break` event via EventBridge.

### Requirement 7: Life Management

**User Story:** As a player, I want to lose a life when the ball exits the bottom edge, so that the game has stakes and a loss condition.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Ball exits the bottom edge of the play area, THE BreakoutScene SHALL call `loseLife` on the current BreakoutState.
2. WHEN a life is lost, THE BreakoutScene SHALL immediately reposition the Ball on top of the Paddle (not just stop velocity).
3. WHEN a life is lost, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit a `lives:update` event via EventBridge with the remaining lives count.
4. WHEN a life is lost, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:life-loss` event via EventBridge.
5. WHEN a life is lost AND the match is not over, THE BreakoutScene SHALL wait a Serve_Delay (500ms–1000ms) before launching the Ball again.
6. WHEN a life is lost, THE BreakoutScene SHALL reset the Ball's speed to Base_Speed.

### Requirement 8: Win and Loss Detection

**User Story:** As a player, I want the match to end with a clear win or loss, so that I know the outcome.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Brick is destroyed, THE BreakoutScene SHALL call `getMatchStatus` on the current BreakoutState to check for win.
2. WHEN `getMatchStatus` returns `'win'`, THE BreakoutScene SHALL stop Ball movement and Paddle input processing.
3. WHEN `getMatchStatus` returns `'win'`, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit a `match:win` event via EventBridge with `{ winner: 'solo' }`.
4. WHEN `getMatchStatus` returns `'win'`, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:win` event via EventBridge.
5. WHEN a life is lost, THE BreakoutScene SHALL call `getMatchStatus` on the current BreakoutState to check for loss.
6. WHEN `getMatchStatus` returns `'loss'`, THE BreakoutScene SHALL stop Ball movement and Paddle input processing.
7. WHEN `getMatchStatus` returns `'loss'`, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit a `match:loss` event via EventBridge with `{ finalScore: state.score }`.
8. WHEN `getMatchStatus` returns `'loss'`, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:loss` event via EventBridge.

### Requirement 9: Pause and Resume

**User Story:** As a player, I want the game to pause and resume when the React shell sends a pause event, so that I can take breaks.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a `match:pause` event with `{ paused: true }` is received from EventBridge, THE BreakoutScene SHALL freeze all physics simulation.
2. WHEN a `match:pause` event with `{ paused: false }` is received from EventBridge, THE BreakoutScene SHALL resume physics simulation from the frozen state.
3. WHILE the match is paused, THE BreakoutScene SHALL ignore Paddle movement input.
4. WHEN the match is paused, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:pause` event via EventBridge.

### Requirement 10: Scene Restart

**User Story:** As a player, I want to restart the match cleanly, so that I can try again without returning to the menu.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a `scene:restart` event is received from EventBridge, THE BreakoutScene SHALL call `this.scene.restart()` to re-initialize the scene.
2. WHEN BreakoutScene restarts, THE BreakoutScene SHALL re-read the SceneLaunchPayload from the SceneLauncher module.
3. WHEN BreakoutScene restarts, THE BreakoutScene SHALL reset all match state: lives to 3, score to 0, regenerate the brick grid, and reposition the Ball on the Paddle.

### Requirement 11: Audio Event Emission

**User Story:** As a player, I want distinct audio cues for all gameplay events, so that I receive auditory feedback during the match.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Ball collides with the Paddle, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:paddle-hit` event via EventBridge.
2. WHEN the Ball collides with a Wall, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:wall-bounce` event via EventBridge.
3. WHEN a Brick is destroyed, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:brick-break` event via EventBridge.
4. WHEN a life is lost, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:life-loss` event via EventBridge.
5. WHEN the player wins, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:win` event via EventBridge.
6. WHEN the player loses all lives, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:loss` event via EventBridge.
7. WHEN the match is paused, THE BreakoutScene SHALL emit an `audio:pause` event via EventBridge.

### Requirement 12: GameView Integration

**User Story:** As a gameplay implementer, I want GameView to launch BreakoutScene when the selected mode is breakout, so that the mode is playable from the app shell.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the selected mode is `'breakout'`, THE GameView SHALL add and start `BreakoutScene` in the Phaser game's `postBoot` callback.
2. WHEN the selected mode is `'breakout'`, THE GameView SHALL pass the SceneLaunchPayload with BreakoutSettings to the SceneLauncher before game creation.
3. THE GameView SHALL subscribe to `lives:update` events from EventBridge and update the Zustand store's matchData.lives field.
4. WHEN a `match:loss` event is received, THE GameView SHALL open the WinLossOverlay with the final score.
